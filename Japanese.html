Japanese has a very useful phrase to say a half hour past the hour such as 1:30 and 2:30. Here is the pattern that you can use: ～じ　はん　です。(It's ~ :30.)

Were you able to hear the phrase? In the lesson video, Reina asks Mr. Takahashi what time school ends, and Mr. Takahashi uses this phrase to answer, saying that school ends at 3:30. れいな：あの、がっこうは　なんじに　おわりますか。(What time does school end?) たかはし：がっこうは　ごご　さんじはんに　 おわります。(It ends at 3:30.)

The lesson video shows you a very useful expression to talk about a class schedule. Using this pattern you can tell what class you have each hour: (Japanese number) じかんめは　 (school subject) です。　　(~ hour is ~)

たかはし：いちじかんめは　りかです。(First hour is science.)

たかはし：にじかんめは　すうがくです。(Second hour is math.)

たかはし：さんじかんめは　こくごです。(Third hour is Japanese.)

たかはし：よじかんめは　えいごです。(Fourth hour is English.)

たかはし：ごじかんめは　じゃかいです。(Fifth hour is social studies.)

～を　かぶります。(Put on something on your head.) ～を　きます。(Put on something on your body from the shoulders down.) ～を　はきます。(Put on something on or over your feet and legs.) ～を　つけます。(Put on small items such as gloves, scarves, watches, earrings, etc.)

なつは　あついです。(It is hot in summer.) ぼうしをかぶります。(We wear hats.) かさをさします。(We use umbrellas.) サンダルを　はきます。(We wear sandals.) Tシャツを　きます。(We wear T-shirts.) ふゆは　さむいです。(It's cold in winter.) コートを きます。(We wear coats.) てぶくろを つけます。(We wear gloves.) ブーツを　はきます。(We wear boots.)
