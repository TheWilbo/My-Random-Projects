Useful Phrases

In the lesson video, Kei describes her country, and at the end of the video, she asks you a question using this expression pattern:

～は　どんなところですか。(What kinds of a place is ~?)

"どんなところですか" literally means "What is the place like?" By adding a topic, you can ask about a particular place.

 

 

Did you get what she asked you? She said:

あなたのくには　どんなところですか。(What kinds of a place is your country?)

 

あなたの/がっこうは/　どんな/ところですか。- What is your school like?

あなたの/くには/　どんな/ところですか。- What is your country like?

あなたの/まちは/　どんな/ところですか。- What is your town like?

Useful Sentence Pattern

In the lesson video, Kei uses a familiar sentence pattern with a combined word to more specifically describe a place:

(Place) は　～まちです。(…is a ~ kind of town.)

(Place) は　～ところです。(…is a ~ kind of place.)

The First 10 Days of the Month

Now that you know how to say the 12 months in Japanese, let's learn how to say the days of a month. You will again use the now-familiar number words. However, the first ten days of a month are a bit tricky because you can't just use the numbers 1-10. You can't use the regular numbers for the 14th, 20th, and 24th days either. Let's see what these days of the month look like and learn how they should sound. Look at the chart below to check how these days should be written and use the flashcards that follow to check the sound of the words. Pay close attention to the 2nd day and the 20th day because they sound very similar.

Useful Sentence Patterns

Now that you know how to ask if someone has brothers and sisters, let's learn how to ask and tell how many brothers and sisters someone has. You can apply the familiar pattern to make questions that fit various situations:

Pattern 1

きょうだいは　なんにん　いますか。(How many brothers and sisters do/does … have?)

きょうだいは　～にん　 います。 (… have/has ~ number of brothers and sisters.)

 

Pattern 2

(specific brother or sister word) は　 なんにん　いますか。(How many older/younger brothers/sisters do you have?)

(specific brother or sister word) は　～にん　 います。(… have/has ~ number of older/younger brothers/sisters.)

 

Pattern 3

きょうだいは　なんにん　いますか。(How many brothers and sisters do/does … have?)

(specific brother or sister word) が　 ～にんいます。(…have/has ~ number of older/younger brothers/sisters.)
